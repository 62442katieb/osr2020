Welcome to the OSR

The OS-SIG Open Science Room (OSR) takes place at the Organisation for Human Brain Mapping (OHBM) annual meeting, this year to be held in Montreal June 26th - 30th.

What is the OSR?

The OSR provides a physical space for meeting attendees to discuss open practices within neuroimaging and science more generally. The OSR exists as welcoming environment which showcases the best of open science and acts as a springboard to incite new ways of thinking and working. This meeting is a key opportunity to connect with others in the open neuroimaging community, learn from eachother and share experiences.

What happens in the OSR?

The OSR hosts short talks, longer 'keynote' talks, software demonstrations, and emergent discussions, all centred around themes relevant to open science practices in our discipline. The content is curated in advance and arranged to avoid clashes with major annual meeting events. All scheduled talks will be selected following an open call, and emergent sessions can be booked via our online calendar.

Talks, dicsussions and demonstrations in the OSR will be fresh, relevant and challenging. We by addressing new and emerging topics, fields and concerns, along with big picture issues in open science.

Who should come to the OSR?

The OSR is a space for everyone. We come with diverse skill sets, expereinces and viewpoints, and none of us are to old to learn something new! We expect visitors of the OSR to come with an open mind comply with the Code of Conduct for the OHBM annual meeting which hosts us. These behaviours are aligned with open science practices of sharing, collaboration, and inclusivity. In the OSR we aim to model the academic research future we wish to be a part of, where we interact with eachother with curiosity, kindness and respect.
